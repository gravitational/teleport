Teleport's on-prem Enterprise deployment provides essential FedRAMP control coverage for the people, workloads, and AI agents operating inside your infrastructure.
Teleport Enterprise is actively used inside FedRAMP-authorized services today, including environments audited against the High baseline.

## Why FedRAMP teams choose Teleport

Teleport solves authentication and authorization within your FedRAMP authorization boundary, giving you extensive coverage across the Access Control (AC) family
along with controls in Audit and Accountability (AU), Identification and Authentication (IA), and System and Communications Protection (SC).
The platform governs who can reach your servers, clusters, databases, web applications, and desktops; what they are authorized to do once connected; and generates detailed audit logs and session recordings.

The Teleport platform manages both human and non-human access to consistently answer the question "who did this, with what authority, and when?" anywhere in your environment.

### People
Granting the right people the right access — and being able to prove it on demand — is the core of every FedRAMP access-control conversation. Findings (and compromises) come from the difference between what's been granted and what **should** have been granted.

With Teleport: 

* IdP integration means everyone gets [Single sign-on](../../sso/sso.mdx) with phishing-resistant MFA that maps directly to your existing group structures.
* One source of truth for authentication AND authorization plus no one-off accounts to revoke when someone leaves.  You manage your IdP accounts and configuration, Teleport follows your lead automatically.
* Shared passwords and break-glass accounts are eliminated; long-lived SSH keys that sneak into repositories or accumulate in `.ssh` directories are replaced with short-lived, role-scoped certificates.

### Workloads
Workload-to-workload access is often the largest and least-visible part of an ATO boundary. As internal services, CI/CD, and batch jobs proliferate, keeping authentication consistent — and the credentials accounted for — becomes a steady drag and constant risk.

With Teleport: 

* Every workload is issued a unique, short-lived, SPIFFE-compatible cryptographic identity through Teleport's [Machine & Workload Identity](../../../machine-workload-identity/machine-workload-identity.mdx). Every workload is now named, and their actions can be tracked just like users.
* Workloads authenticate using short-lived cryptographic credentials in place of static API keys and service-account passwords — mTLS where the destination supports it (other workloads, databases, internal services), and federated short-lived tokens for cloud APIs.
* Static API keys and service-account credentials are replaced with auto-rotating identities under the same access and audit controls as your people.
* Internal access between hosts gains consistent authentication and authorization that's centrally managed.

### AI Agents
Engineering is wiring AI agents into internal systems faster than compliance teams can track them. Without an identity attached to each agent, an LLM querying your production database has no attributable record in your audit trail, and represents a growing risk of unauthorized access.

With Teleport: 

* Agents receive their own identity through Teleport's [Agentic Identity Framework](../../../agentic-identity-framework.mdx).  Engineers have a standard approach for agent authentication to replace ad hoc, inconsistent, and insecure access controls.
* Agentic Identity brings agents under the same RBAC model as your users and workloads. Agent sprawl and unattributed activity become visible and enforceable at the platform layer.
* Access to MCP servers, databases, and internal APIs is brokered, authorized, and audited by Teleport's identity-aware proxy, adding enhanced logging and additional access control filters.

### FIPS 140-2 & FIPS 140-3 support

<Admonition type="warning" title="FIPS 140-2 module sunsets September 21, 2026">

Teleport releases prior to 17.7.3 use BoringCrypto [CMVP certificate 4407](https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cryptographic-module-validation-program/certificate/4407), which sunsets September 21, 2026.
Upgrade to Teleport 17.7.3+ or 18.0.0+ to move to the FIPS 140-3 validated BoringCrypto module ([CMVP certificate 4735](https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cryptographic-module-validation-program/certificate/4735)).

</Admonition>

Teleport Enterprise's built-in FIPS support enables a FIPS 140-validated cryptographic module on the platform to satisfy the cryptographic requirements of NIST SP 800-53 controls SC-13 (Cryptographic Protection) and IA-7 (Cryptographic Module Authentication).

- **FIPS 140-3**: Teleport releases from 17.7.3+ and 18.0.0+ use BoringCrypto tag `fips-20220613` ([CMVP certificate 4735](https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cryptographic-module-validation-program/certificate/4735), FIPS 140-3)
- **FIPS 140-2**: Teleport releases prior to 17.7.3 use BoringCrypto tag `fips-20210429` ([CMVP certificate 4407](https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cryptographic-module-validation-program/certificate/4407), FIPS 140-2) - sunsets September 21, 2026.

A detailed list of cryptographic algorithms used in FIPS mode is available in the [FAQ](./faq.mdx#which-cryptographic-algorithms-does-fips-mode-use).
For more information on BoringCrypto's FIPS validation, see Google's [BoringCrypto FIPS module documentation](https://boringssl.googlesource.com/boringssl/+/HEAD/crypto/fipsmodule/FIPS.md).

In practice, a Teleport deployment in your FedRAMP environment standardizes access control across your ATO boundary that would otherwise be a collection of bespoke, ad hoc services, password and secret management, logging and management functions -
the sort of ad hoc implementations that are almost guaranteed to have security gaps leading to vulnerabilities and control findings.

With Teleport, you and your 3PAO work through dozens of control implementations in one sitting - presenting evidence, demonstrating capabilities, and offering short, simple, accurate explanations.
Your team demonstrates consistent, well-considered, compliant security, and your assessors get the information they need in one place — the unified UI, the session-termination locks, and the session recording system are typically the demonstrations assessors find most efficient.
